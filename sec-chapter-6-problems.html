    Example 6. Volume of Revolution.

    Consider the region above \(y=x^2\) and below \(y=7x\text{,}\) graphed below. The axes are intentionally left blank, so you may need to use algebra to determine information about where the graphs intersect.
    🔗
    1. Assuming you will be finding a volume of revolution for this region rotated around the \(x\)-axis, draw a typical slice on the picture above.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. Set up, but do not evaluate, the definite integral that gives the volume of this region when it is rotated around the \(x\)-axis.
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Describe in a few sentences how the definite integral that you gave in part 2 represents the volume of revolution. You do not need to describe anything about the bounds of integration. You may receive some credit for indicating how you plugged expressions into a formula you had memorized, but in order to receive full credit, you must justify your answer using geometry. You may draw additional pictures as part of your description.
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. 🔗
    2. First, find the intersctions:
      \begin{align*} x^2 \amp = 7x\\ x^2 - 7x \amp = 0\\ x (x-7) \amp = 0\\ x = 0 \quad \amp \text{or} \quad x = 7 \end{align*}
      Use these points of intersection to set up the definite integral. All of the following forms are equivalent and would be correct solutions:
      \begin{align*} \amp \int_0^7 ( \pi(7x)^2 - \pi(x^2)^2 ) \; dx\\ \amp \int_0^7 \pi ( (7x)^2 - (x^2)^2 ) \; dx\\ \amp \int_0^7 \pi ( 49x^2 - x^4 ) \; dx \end{align*}
      🔗
      🔗
    3. Upon rotating a slice (from part 1) around the \(x\)-axis, the trace is a washer-like shape. The area of the full washer (without the hole) is \(\pi(\text{radius})^2\text{.}\) In this case, \(\text{radius}=7x\text{,}\) so \(\pi(7x)^2\text{.}\) Similarly, the area of the hole is \(\pi(x^2)^2\text{.}\) Subtracting the area of the hole from the area of the full washer gives the area of the washer slice -- the correct integrand.
      🔗

    Example 7. Work Pumping Water, Version A.

    The work required to pump water out of a tank is given by the following integral:
    \begin{equation*} W = \int_a^b A(h)\, D(h)\, \delta(h)\, dh \end{equation*}
    which is defined by the following four important components:
    • \(a\) and \(b\) are constant values representing the appropriate bounds of integration,
      🔗
    • \(A(h)\) is a function that gives the area of a slice,
      🔗
    • \(D(h)\) is a function that gives the distance a slice must be lifted, and
      🔗
    • \(\delta(h)\) is a function that gives the density of the fluid (in weight per unit volume) within a slice.
      🔗
    🔗
    1. Determine values or formulas for the four important components exactly for the following specific problem:
      🔗
      Problem: A rectangular tank is oriented upright. The height of the tank is \(13\) ft, and the length and width of the tank are \(2\) ft by \(9\) ft. The tank is full of water (which has a constant weight density of \(62.4\) pounds per cubic foot), and the water is to be pumped out of the top of the tank.
      🔗
      To receive full credit, you must draw a picture with a typical slice and clearly label what distance is represented by \(h\text{,}\) since there are multiple valid ways of setting up the problem. You only need to define the components; you do not need to evaluate the integral itself.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. The sub-parts below are unrelated. For each scenario described below, justify in complete sentences which of the four important components would be affected in the new context. Describe all components affected; there may be more than one.
      • One of the length/width dimensions of the tank is made smaller.
        🔗
        🔗
      • Only the top few feet of water are to be pumped out of the tank.
        🔗
        🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. Let \(h\) be the distance down from the top of the tank.
      • \(a=0\text{,}\) \(b=13\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle A(h)=2\cdot9=18\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle D(h)=h\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle \delta(h)=62.4\)
        🔗
      The total work required is:
      \begin{equation*} \int_0^{13} 18 (62.4) h \; dh \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      • \(A(h)\) would change because the area \(A(h)=\text{length}\times\text{width}\text{,}\) so shrinking the length/width will shrink the area.
        🔗
        🔗
      • In the setup above, \(a\) and \(b\) correspond to the values of \(h\) at the top and the bottom of the tank. Limiting to pumping the top few feet would change the value of \(b\) (making it smaller).
        🔗
        🔗
      🔗
      🔗

    Example 8. Work Pumping Water, Version B.

    The work required to pump water out of a tank is given by the following integral:
    \begin{equation*} W = \int_a^b A(h)\, D(h)\, \delta(h)\, dh \end{equation*}
    which is defined by the following four important components:
    • \(a\) and \(b\) are constant values representing the appropriate bounds of integration,
      🔗
    • \(A(h)\) is a function that gives the area of a slice,
      🔗
    • \(D(h)\) is a function that gives the distance a slice must be lifted, and
      🔗
    • \(\delta(h)\) is a function that gives the density of the fluid (in weight per unit volume) within a slice.
      🔗
    🔗
    1. Determine values or formulas for the four important components exactly for the following specific problem:
      🔗
      Problem: A cylindrical tank is oriented upright (so that its circular base is on the ground). The height of the tank is \(12\) ft and the radius is \(4\) ft. The tank is full of water (which has a constant weight density of \(62.4\) pounds per cubic foot), and the water is to be pumped out of the top of the tank.
      🔗
      To receive full credit, you must draw a picture with a typical slice and clearly label what distance is represented by \(h\text{,}\) since there are multiple valid ways of setting up the problem. You only need to define the components; you do not need to evaluate the integral itself.
      🔗
      🔗
    2. The sub-parts below are unrelated. For each scenario described below, justify in complete sentences which of the four important components would be affected in the new context. Describe all components affected; there may be more than one.
      • The radius of the tank is made smaller.
        🔗
        🔗
      • The tank is instead filled with two or more fluids which have separated so the denser fluids are closer to the bottom of the tank.
        🔗
        🔗
      🔗
      🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    1. Let \(h\) be the distance up from the bottom of the tank, as in the picture above.
      • \(a=0\text{,}\) \(b=12\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle A(h)=\pi r^2=\pi\cdot4^2=16\pi \quad \text{(square feet)}\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle D(h)=12-h \quad \text{(ft)}\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle \delta(h)=62.4 \quad \text{(pounds per cubic foot)}\)
        🔗
      The total work required is:
      \begin{equation*} \int_0^{12} (16\pi) (12-h) (62.4) \; dh \end{equation*}
      🔗
      An alternative solution may be given where \(h\) is now the distance down from the top of the tank, as in the picture below.
      🔗
      • \(a=0\text{,}\) \(b=12\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle A(h)=\pi r^2=\pi\cdot4^2=16\pi \quad \text{(square feet)}\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle D(h)=h \quad \text{(ft)}\)
        🔗
      • \(\displaystyle \delta(h)=62.4 \quad \text{(pounds per cubic foot)}\)
        🔗
      In this case, the total work required would be given by:
      \begin{equation*} \int_0^{12} (16\pi) (h) (62.4) \; dh \end{equation*}
      🔗
      🔗
      • The radius of the tank shrinking means \(A(h)\) decreases, since \(A(h)=\pi r^2\text{.}\) If the radius shrinks, so does \(A(h)\text{.}\) None of the other components are affected: \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(D(h)\) do not change since the water is still to be moved the same height, and \(\delta(h)\) is unchanged since the liquid is unchanged.
        🔗
        🔗
      • The fluids separating means only \(\delta(h)\) is affected, although we do not have enough information to say how. This is because the fluid density varies, and the density may now depend on where the slice is taken. Since the tank itself does not change, none of the other components are affected.
        🔗
        🔗
      🔗
      🔗
